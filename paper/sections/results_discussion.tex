\section{Results and Discussion}
\label{sec:results_discussion}

\subsection{Main Results}
\label{subsec:main_quantization_results}

Table~\ref{tab:main_results} reports Top-1 accuracy, accuracy drop relative to FP32, activation reconstruction MSE, and logit MSE on CIFAR-10.

\begin{table*}[t]
\centering
\caption{Main CIFAR-10 results. Activation MSE and logit MSE characterize simulated quantization error.}
\label{tab:main_results}
\begin{tabular}{l c c l l c c c c}
\hline
\textbf{Method} & \textbf{W bits} & \textbf{A bits} & \textbf{Act. site} & \textbf{Act. granularity} & \textbf{Top-1 (\%)} & \textbf{Drop (pp)} & \textbf{Act. MSE} & \textbf{Logit MSE} \\
\hline
FP32 & 32 & 32 & none & none & 94.1300 & 0.0000 & 0.00000000 & 0.00000000 \\
INT8-MinMax & 8 & 8 & conv\_linear\_output & per\_tensor & 94.1100 & 0.0200 & 0.00005792 & 0.00910544 \\
INT4-MinMax & 4 & 4 & post\_relu & per\_tensor\_per\_relu\_module & 88.2000 & 5.9300 & 0.00273660 & 1.84912006 \\
INT4-P99.9 & 4 & 4 & post\_relu & per\_tensor\_per\_relu\_module & 92.9700 & 1.1600 & 0.00035606 & 0.46350010 \\
INT4-MSE-Selected & 4 & 4 & post\_relu & per\_tensor\_per\_relu\_module & 92.8600 & 1.2700 & 0.00034846 & 0.41728575 \\
\hline
\end{tabular}
\end{table*}

INT8-MinMax remains close to FP32, while INT4-MinMax drops to 88.2000\%, a 5.9300 percentage-point decrease. In this evaluated CIFAR-10 ResNet-18 run, both clipping variants reduce this degradation: INT4-P99.9 reaches 92.9700\%, and INT4-MSE-Selected reaches 92.8600\%. Thus, INT4-MSE-Selected improves over INT4-MinMax, but it does not exceed the fixed INT4-P99.9 baseline in Top-1 accuracy. The result therefore supports mitigation of INT4-MinMax degradation in the implemented setting, not a claim that MSE-selected clipping is uniformly better than fixed percentile clipping.

\subsection{Error and Layer-wise Analysis}
\label{subsec:error_and_layerwise_analysis}

\begin{figure}[t]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{outputs/figures/error_metrics.png}
\caption{Activation reconstruction MSE and logit MSE of the quantized methods.}
\label{fig:error_metrics}
\end{figure}

Compared with INT4-MinMax, INT4-MSE-Selected reduces activation reconstruction MSE from 0.00273660 to 0.00034846 and logit MSE from 1.84912006 to 0.41728575. It also has slightly lower error metrics than INT4-P99.9, although this does not translate into the highest Top-1 accuracy. This mismatch suggests that calibration reconstruction MSE is useful for analyzing simulated quantization behavior but is not a complete substitute for test-set accuracy.

\begin{figure}[t]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{outputs/figures/layerwise_mse.png}
\caption{Layer-wise activation reconstruction MSE for INT4-MSE-Selected. Annotations indicate the selected percentile at each observed post-ReLU activation site.}
\label{fig:layerwise_mse}
\end{figure}

Fig.~\ref{fig:layerwise_mse} shows that selected percentiles and reconstruction errors vary across observed post-ReLU activation sites, confirming that the implemented calibration rule is applied layer-wise within the predefined candidate set. This should not be interpreted as continuous globally optimal clipping, because the search is restricted to \(\{99.0,99.5,99.9,99.95,100.0\}\). Qualitatively, clipping can reduce INT4-MinMax error because rare large post-ReLU values stretch the MinMax range, while high-percentile or MSE-selected clipping trades limited saturation for lower rounding error on common values.

\subsection{Limitations}
\label{subsec:results_limitations}

The current result is based on one dataset, one model, one random seed, and one configuration. No multi-seed study, calibration-size sweep, multi-architecture evaluation, QAT comparison, real INT4 inference kernel, hardware latency, throughput, energy measurement, or deployment speedup is reported. A specific failure case in this run is that lower activation reconstruction MSE and logit MSE for INT4-MSE-Selected do not lead to higher Top-1 accuracy than INT4-P99.9. The results should therefore be viewed as a controlled comparison among the implemented baselines rather than a comprehensive PTQ benchmark.
