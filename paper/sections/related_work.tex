\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related_work}

PTQ reduces numerical precision after training and typically uses calibration to determine quantization parameters. Surveys and white papers summarize common PTQ settings, calibration rules, and fake-quantized evaluation procedures \cite{gholami2021survey,nagel2021whitepaper,krishnamoorthi2018quantizing}. Integer-only inference further motivates practical low-precision deployment schemes \cite{jacob2018quantization}. This paper does not attempt to compete with comprehensive PTQ methods. Instead, it examines a simple finite-candidate activation clipping rule.

Low-bit CNN quantization can reduce memory footprint and arithmetic cost in implementations that support the corresponding low-precision execution. However, this paper does not measure hardware latency, throughput, or energy. In addition, 4-bit PTQ is more sensitive to scale and clipping choices than 8-bit PTQ \cite{banner2018post,choukroun2019low}. Activation outliers are especially relevant under MinMax calibration because rare large values can determine the full quantization range. Prior work has explored activation clipping and outlier handling, including parameterized activation clipping and outlier channel splitting \cite{choi2018pact,zhao2019outlier}. The method in this paper follows this clipping direction but uses no retraining or learnable clipping parameters. Each post-ReLU activation threshold is selected from a predefined percentile set according to calibration reconstruction MSE.
