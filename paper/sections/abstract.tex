\begin{abstract}
Post-training quantization (PTQ) is an attractive compression technique for deploying convolutional neural networks on resource-constrained edge devices, because it can be applied after FP32 training without retraining. However, INT4 PTQ is more sensitive than INT8 PTQ to activation range calibration. In particular, MinMax calibration can be affected by rare large post-ReLU activations, which enlarge the quantization interval and increase reconstruction error for the dense activation region. This paper studies Layer-wise MSE-Selected Activation Clipping for simulated INT4 PTQ. For each observed post-ReLU activation site, the method searches a predefined percentile candidate set and selects the clipping threshold that minimizes calibration-set activation reconstruction mean squared error. Experiments are conducted on CIFAR-10 using a ResNet-18-CIFAR classifier, comparing FP32, INT8-MinMax, INT4-MinMax, INT4-P99.9, and INT4-MSE-Selected settings. INT4-MinMax drops Top-1 accuracy from 94.13\% to 88.20\%, while INT4-MSE-Selected reaches 92.86\% and reduces activation/logit reconstruction error relative to INT4-MinMax. The results suggest that layer-wise clipping is useful for reducing simulated INT4 PTQ degradation and revealing activation-layer sensitivity.
\end{abstract}
