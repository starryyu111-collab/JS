\begin{abstract}
Low-bit post-training quantization (PTQ) is often motivated by potential CNN deployment on resource-constrained edge devices; however, this paper focuses only on simulation-based PTQ evaluation with fake quantization and dequantization, without implementing a real INT4 inference kernel. We study Layer-wise MSE-Selected Activation Clipping, a simple calibration-time rule for simulated INT4 PTQ. For each post-ReLU activation site, the method evaluates a predefined percentile set on a CIFAR-10 calibration subset and selects the threshold with the lowest activation reconstruction MSE. Experiments on CIFAR-10 using a CIFAR-style ResNet-18 compare FP32, INT8-MinMax, INT4-MinMax, INT4-P99.9, and INT4-MSE-Selected. In this evaluated setting, INT4-MSE-Selected improves Top-1 accuracy over INT4-MinMax and reduces activation reconstruction MSE and logit MSE, but it does not outperform the fixed INT4-P99.9 baseline in accuracy.
\end{abstract}
