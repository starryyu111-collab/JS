\begin{abstract}
Low-bit post-training quantization (PTQ) is an attractive compression technique for deploying convolutional neural networks on resource-constrained edge devices, but INT4 activation quantization is sensitive to activation outliers. With ordinary MinMax calibration, rare large activation values can expand the quantization range and increase rounding error for more common values. This paper studies a simple calibration-time clipping strategy, called Layer-wise MSE-Selected Activation Clipping, for simulated INT4 PTQ. The method collects post-ReLU activations on a CIFAR-10 training calibration subset and, for each activation site, evaluates a predefined set of percentile thresholds. The threshold with the lowest activation reconstruction mean squared error is selected for that layer. Experiments are conducted on CIFAR-10 using a CIFAR-style ResNet-18 model and compare FP32, INT8-MinMax, INT4-MinMax, INT4-P99.9, and INT4-MSE-Selected. The results show that INT4-MinMax causes a large accuracy drop, while clipping-based INT4 methods substantially reduce this degradation. INT4-MSE-Selected improves Top-1 accuracy over INT4-MinMax and reduces both activation reconstruction MSE and logit MSE, although it does not outperform the fixed INT4-P99.9 baseline in accuracy.
\end{abstract}
