\begin{abstract}
Low-bit post-training quantization (PTQ) is often motivated by CNN deployment on resource-constrained edge devices. This paper, however, focuses only on simulation-based PTQ evaluation with fake quantization and dequantization, and does not implement a real INT4 inference kernel. We evaluate Layer-wise MSE-Selected Activation Clipping, a simple calibration-time rule for simulated INT4 PTQ. The method targets post-ReLU activations, where rare large values may enlarge the MinMax range and reduce the effective resolution for common values. For each post-ReLU activation site, it evaluates a predefined percentile set on a CIFAR-10 calibration subset and selects the threshold that minimizes activation reconstruction MSE. Experiments on CIFAR-10 with a CIFAR-style ResNet-18 compare FP32, INT8-MinMax, INT4-MinMax, INT4-P99.9, and INT4-MSE-Selected. In the evaluated setting, INT4-MSE-Selected improves Top-1 accuracy over INT4-MinMax and reduces activation reconstruction MSE and logit MSE, but it does not outperform the fixed INT4-P99.9 baseline in accuracy. These results support a controlled analysis of calibration behavior rather than a hardware speedup claim.
\end{abstract}
