\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:introduction}

Post-training quantization (PTQ) is a practical way to reduce CNN inference precision after training, but aggressive INT4 quantization is sensitive to calibration choices. In activation quantization, rare large post-ReLU values can dominate MinMax calibration, expand the quantization range, and reduce the effective resolution for more common activation values. Activation clipping can mitigate this effect by trading limited saturation of large values for smaller rounding error on the bulk of the activation distribution.

This paper studies a calibration-time clipping method for simulated INT4 PTQ, called Layer-wise MSE-Selected Activation Clipping. For each post-ReLU activation site, the method evaluates a predefined set of percentile thresholds, fake-quantizes and dequantizes the collected calibration activations, and selects the candidate with the lowest activation reconstruction MSE. The search is discrete and limited to the predefined candidate set; it is not a continuous global optimization of the clipping threshold.

The scope is intentionally controlled. We evaluate simulated PTQ on CIFAR-10 using a CIFAR-style ResNet-18 model and compare FP32, INT8-MinMax, INT4-MinMax, INT4-P99.9, and INT4-MSE-Selected. The implementation uses fake quantization and dequantization to study accuracy and reconstruction error, without reporting real hardware latency or deployment speedup.

The main contributions are:
\begin{itemize}
    \item a controlled simulated PTQ pipeline for CIFAR-10 CNN quantization;
    \item a layer-wise MSE-selected activation clipping strategy for INT4 post-ReLU activation quantization;
    \item an analysis of accuracy, reconstruction error, logit consistency, and layer-wise clipping behavior under the implemented baselines.
\end{itemize}
