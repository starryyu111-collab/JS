\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:introduction}

Convolutional neural networks (CNNs) are widely used for image classification, but their direct deployment on edge devices can be constrained by limited computation, memory capacity, and data movement budget. Model compression is therefore an important practical direction for edge-oriented CNN deployment. Among different compression techniques, post-training quantization (PTQ) is attractive because it can be applied after FP32 training without back-propagation or model retraining.

INT8 PTQ is commonly used as a practical low-precision setting and often serves as a sanity-check baseline for a quantization pipeline. Moving from INT8 to INT4, however, is more challenging because only a small number of integer levels are available for representing weights and activations. In this setting, activation quantization becomes especially sensitive to calibration. A simple MinMax calibration rule preserves the largest observed activation value, but rare large activation values can stretch the quantization range. For post-ReLU activations, this may make the quantization step size too coarse for the dense region near zero, leading to increased reconstruction error and classification accuracy degradation.

Activation clipping provides a direct way to address this issue. Instead of always preserving the maximum calibration activation, clipping sets an upper bound for the activation range. A smaller clipping threshold can reduce rounding error for the majority of activation values, but it may also introduce clipping error by saturating large activations. Therefore, the clipping threshold controls a trade-off between clipping error and rounding error. This trade-off is particularly relevant for INT4 PTQ, where the activation integer range is very limited.

This paper studies a simple calibration-time method named Layer-wise MSE-Selected Activation Clipping for simulated INT4 PTQ. For each observed post-ReLU activation site, calibration activations are collected from a subset of the CIFAR-10 training set. The method evaluates a predefined percentile candidate set and selects the candidate whose fake-quantized--dequantized activations produce the smallest reconstruction mean squared error (MSE) for that layer. The selected threshold is then fixed during evaluation. The method is not quantization-aware training (QAT), does not update model weights, and does not search a continuous threshold space; it is MSE-selected only within the predefined candidate percentile set.

The study is conducted as a controlled CIFAR-10 case study using a ResNet-18-CIFAR classifier. The proposed method is compared with an FP32 reference, an INT8-MinMax sanity-check baseline, an INT4-MinMax baseline, and a fixed-percentile INT4-P99.9 clipping baseline. The goal is not to claim state-of-the-art performance or real hardware acceleration. Instead, the paper focuses on whether calibration-time activation clipping can reduce INT4 MinMax degradation and reveal layer-wise sensitivity in a simulated PTQ setting.

The main contributions of this paper are summarized as follows:
\begin{itemize}
    \item A controlled simulated PTQ evaluation pipeline is constructed for CIFAR-10 / ResNet-18-CIFAR, including FP32, INT8-MinMax, INT4-MinMax, and INT4 fixed-percentile clipping baselines.
    \item A layer-wise MSE-selected activation clipping method is introduced for INT4 PTQ, where each post-ReLU activation threshold is selected from a small predefined percentile candidate set using calibration-set reconstruction MSE.
    \item Experimental analysis shows that activation clipping reduces the degradation of INT4-MinMax PTQ in this case study, while layer-wise MSE-selected clipping reduces activation/logit reconstruction error and reveals non-uniform layer sensitivity.
\end{itemize}
