\section{Experiments}
\label{sec:experiments}

All experiments are conducted on CIFAR-10 using a CIFAR-style ResNet-18 model, denoted as \texttt{resnet18\_cifar}. A trained FP32 checkpoint is used as the starting point, and all quantized variants are produced from this fixed model. Accuracy is evaluated on the full CIFAR-10 test set.

For calibration, 1024 CIFAR-10 training samples are drawn with seed 42 and mini-batch size 128. The calibration subset is sampled from the training split only and is used only to collect activation statistics and determine clipping thresholds; it is not used for reporting classification accuracy, and no model weights are updated during calibration.

This work evaluates simulated PTQ with fake quantization and dequantization in floating-point computation. No real INT4 hardware kernel or latency measurement is included. The compared settings are FP32, INT8-MinMax, INT4-MinMax, INT4-P99.9, and INT4-MSE-Selected. INT8-MinMax uses signed symmetric per-channel weights in $[-127,127]$ and uint8 affine activations in $[0,255]$, while the INT4 variants use signed symmetric per-channel weights in $[-7,7]$ and post-ReLU uint4 activations in $[0,15]$. Because INT8-MinMax and the INT4 variants use different activation sites and granularities, INT8-MinMax is used mainly as a reference sanity baseline; the controlled calibration comparison is among INT4-MinMax, INT4-P99.9, and INT4-MSE-Selected. The primary metric is Top-1 accuracy on the CIFAR-10 test set. Activation reconstruction MSE is computed between FP32 post-ReLU activations and their fake-quantized/dequantized counterparts, while logit MSE compares FP32 and quantized model logits on the evaluation data.
