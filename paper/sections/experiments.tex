\section{Experiments}
\label{sec:experiments}

All experiments are conducted on CIFAR-10 using a CIFAR-style ResNet-18 model, denoted as \texttt{resnet18\_cifar}. A trained FP32 checkpoint is used as the starting point, and all quantized variants are produced from this fixed model. Accuracy is evaluated on the full CIFAR-10 test set.

For calibration, 1024 CIFAR-10 training samples are drawn with seed 42 and mini-batch size 128. The calibration subset is used only to collect activation statistics and determine clipping thresholds; it is not used for reporting classification accuracy, and no model weights are updated during calibration.

This work evaluates simulated PTQ with fake quantization and dequantization in floating-point computation. No real INT4 hardware kernel or latency measurement is included. The compared settings are FP32, INT8-MinMax, INT4-MinMax, INT4-P99.9, and INT4-MSE-Selected. INT4 activations are quantized at post-ReLU sites using per-tensor per-module calibration; INT4 weights use per-channel symmetric simulated quantization. The primary metric is Top-1 accuracy on the CIFAR-10 test set, and activation reconstruction MSE and logit MSE are reported to characterize simulated quantization error.
