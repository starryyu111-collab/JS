\section{Experiments}
\label{sec:experiments}

The experiments use CIFAR-10 and a CIFAR-style ResNet-18 model, denoted as \texttt{resnet18\_cifar}. A trained FP32 checkpoint is used as the starting point, and all quantized variants are produced from this fixed model. Accuracy is evaluated on the full CIFAR-10 test set.

Calibration uses 1024 CIFAR-10 training samples drawn with seed 42 and a mini-batch size of 128. The calibration subset is sampled only from the training split and is used only to collect activation statistics and determine clipping thresholds. It is not used for reporting classification accuracy, and no model weights are updated during calibration.

This work evaluates simulated PTQ with fake quantization and dequantization in floating-point computation. No real INT4 hardware kernel or latency measurement is included. The compared settings are FP32, INT8-MinMax, INT4-MinMax, INT4-P99.9, and INT4-MSE-Selected. INT8-MinMax uses signed symmetric per-channel weights in $[-127,127]$ and uint8 affine activations in $[0,255]$, whereas the INT4 variants use signed symmetric per-channel weights in $[-7,7]$ and post-ReLU uint4 activations in $[0,15]$. Because INT8-MinMax and the INT4 variants use different activation sites and granularities, INT8-MinMax serves mainly as a sanity-check reference baseline. The controlled calibration comparison is among INT4-MinMax, INT4-P99.9, and INT4-MSE-Selected.

The primary metric is Top-1 accuracy on the CIFAR-10 test set. Activation reconstruction MSE is computed between FP32 post-ReLU activations and their fake-quantized/dequantized counterparts. Logit MSE compares FP32 and quantized model logits on the evaluation data.
