\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

This paper evaluated simulated INT4 PTQ for a CIFAR-style ResNet-18 on CIFAR-10, focusing on calibration-time activation clipping. In the evaluated setting, INT4-MSE-Selected improves Top-1 accuracy over INT4-MinMax and reduces activation reconstruction MSE and logit MSE. However, it does not outperform the fixed INT4-P99.9 baseline in accuracy. The method should therefore be interpreted as a conservative layer-wise calibration rule for mitigating INT4-MinMax degradation in the implemented simulated PTQ setting, not as a universally superior clipping method. Future work should evaluate additional architectures and implement real low-precision inference kernels to connect simulated metrics with measured hardware behavior.
