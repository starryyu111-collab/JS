\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

This paper evaluated simulated INT4 PTQ for a CIFAR-style ResNet-18 on CIFAR-10, focusing on activation outlier suppression through calibration-time clipping. The proposed INT4-MSE-Selected method selects a post-ReLU activation threshold for each observed site by minimizing reconstruction MSE over a predefined percentile set.

The results show that INT4-MSE-Selected improves Top-1 accuracy over INT4-MinMax and reduces both activation reconstruction MSE and logit MSE. However, it does not outperform the fixed INT4-P99.9 baseline in Top-1 accuracy in this experiment. The method should therefore be interpreted as a conservative layer-wise calibration strategy that mitigates INT4-MinMax degradation, not as a universally superior clipping method.

The main limitations are that the study uses one dataset, one model, one random seed, and simulated PTQ only. No real INT4 inference kernel, hardware measurement, multi-architecture evaluation, QAT comparison, or comprehensive comparison with stronger PTQ methods is included. Future work should test the method across additional architectures and connect simulated quantization metrics with measured hardware behavior.
